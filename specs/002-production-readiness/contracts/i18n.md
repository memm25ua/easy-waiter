# Contract: English And Spanish Internationalization

## Scope

The production product supports English (`en`) and Spanish (`es`) for product
interface text across public marketing, account, onboarding, customer ordering,
AI waiter, staff orders, manager menus, analytics, staff invitations, access
states, health/smoke states, and operational guidance.

Restaurant-provided content is not automatically translated. Menu item names,
menu descriptions, staff notes, customer notes, uploaded menu content, and
free-form lead messages remain as entered.

## Locale Resolution

Locale must resolve in this order:

1. Explicit language selection made by the user in the current browser/session.
2. Authenticated account language preference when the user is signed in.
3. Anonymous table or marketing session preference when available.
4. Browser/user locale when it starts with `en` or `es`.
5. English (`en`) fallback.

Unsupported locales must never expose raw translation keys or implementation
details.

## Language Selection

Every primary public, customer, account, staff, and manager surface must expose
a clear way to switch between English and Spanish.

Switching language must:

- Preserve the current task state where possible.
- Avoid dropping form values, selected menu options, cart proposals, staff
  notes, or menu review edits.
- Update navigation, labels, actions, validation messages, empty states,
  success states, failure states, and status copy.
- Keep layout stable on mobile when Spanish strings are longer than English
  strings.

## Translatable Product Copy

The translation catalog must cover:

- Global navigation and language selector labels
- Landing page headings, body copy, CTAs, lead form labels, validation, and
  success/failure messages
- Sign-up, sign-in, sign-out, invitation acceptance, onboarding, and no-access
  states
- Manager dashboard, menu import/review/publish, staff invitations, analytics,
  and order board states
- Customer menu browsing, order submission, order status, inactive/expired
  table-session states, and AI waiter copy
- AI fallback, escalation, confirmation, and decline guidance
- Health, deployment smoke, and operational guidance visible to operators

## AI Language Contract

AI waiter and AI menu assistance flows receive the selected locale for
user-visible phrasing. Locale does not change tenant scope, approved menu
context, escalation rules, or deterministic confirmation rules.

Audit events should record the locale used for AI replies, fallback messages,
and escalation guidance.

## Required Tests

- Locale resolution selects explicit preference before browser locale.
- Browser/user locale `es-*` defaults to Spanish; `en-*` defaults to English;
  unsupported locales default to English.
- Public landing conversion can be completed in English and Spanish.
- Account sign-up/sign-in/onboarding/invitation flows can be completed in
  English and Spanish.
- Customer table ordering, AI waiter fallback/escalation, and order status can
  be completed in English and Spanish.
- Manager/staff dashboard, menu review, order status update, staff invitation,
  and analytics flows can be completed in English and Spanish.
- Missing translation keys show a safe fallback and fail test coverage before
  release.
- Spanish labels do not overflow compact mobile controls in the covered
  responsive viewports.
